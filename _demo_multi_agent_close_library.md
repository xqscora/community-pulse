## Maya Chen — high school student

**Top drives:** curiosity=0.74, achievement=0.52, expression=0.48

Okay, wow. That’s… crushing. My first thought is just, *gone*. The library is… it's where I go when I need to really dig into something, you know? Not just skimming Wikipedia, but actually researching, looking at the old books. It’s where I found out about the robotics competitions, too. It feels… small, like a little piece of everything I care about is just being taken away. My stomach is doing that weird fluttery thing, like I’ve just unexpectedly gotten a bad grade. 

Honestly, the biggest thing pulling me in is the idea of *access*. The library gave me so much – the ability to explore without worrying about cost, quiet space to think, and a place to actually learn. It’s not just about books, it’s about opportunity, and this feels like a serious loss of that. I keep seeing the shelves in my mind’s eye, and it’s… unsettling. 

My biggest worry is that I won’t have anywhere to go to really focus on the internship applications. I was hoping to spend a few hours a week there, researching companies and checking out resources. Now, I just… I don't know. It's hard to imagine how I'll find that same kind of support. I really hope there’s some way to keep the space open, maybe through fundraising or something. 

I'm going to start emailing the library board tomorrow. Just a polite request to let them know how many people rely on it, you know?  Maybe if enough people speak up, they’ll reconsider.

## Elena Ruiz — retired teacher

**Top drives:** social=0.64, fairness=0.57, curiosity=0.41

Oh, my goodness. This is… terrible. The library. It’s just… gone? I’ve been volunteering there, shelving the history section, really getting to know Mrs. Henderson – she always has the most fascinating old maps. It feels so… sudden. Honestly, my chest feels a little tight, a familiar sort of settling unease. It’s not a panic, not exactly, just a deep, quiet disappointment. 

The biggest thing, I think, is the *fairness* of it all. The town council always talks about balancing budgets but this feels like a straight-up cut to the people who need it most – the kids, the seniors, folks who don’t have internet access. And then, I keep thinking about all the wonderful programs they had, the reading groups, the children’s story time… It’s just… sad. 

My worry is that it’ll make things harder for young Leo. His mother works such long hours, and the library was his escape. I hope—I truly hope—that someone can find a way to keep the children’s books, at least. Maybe we could organize a little fundraising effort at the community center? Something to show the council we care, that we haven’t just given up.
